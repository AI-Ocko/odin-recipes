Ramen Recipe

Description

Traditional, Shoyu ramen, cooked in pork neck bone broth

Ingredients

  • 6 lb Pork Neck Bone
  • 2 lb Pork Shoulder or Pork Belly
  • 1 Large Leek
  • 2 ginger roots
  • 1 dried konbu or kelp
  • 2 bulbs of garlic
  • 1 & 1/2 tbsps. chicken powder
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 & 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 3/4 cup sake
  • 3/4 cup mirin
  • 2 Eggs

Steps

  1. Soak the pork bones in cold water for 30 minutes to an hour
  2. Discard the water, bring a pot of new water to a boil, and blanche the pork bones for 5-7 mintues
  3. Drain and discard the water
  4. Thoroughly wash each neck bone, and place into pot boiling water over high heat
  5. Add each fragrance vegetable - Sliced Ginger - Leek chopped in half and cleaned - 2 bulbs of garlic cut in half - Dried kombu or kelp. Be sure to remove any excess white stuff - 1 & 1/2 tbsp. of chicken powder
  6. Skim off excess material that accumulates at the top. Be sure not to stir the soup as the scum could sink back to the bottom
  7. Afterwards, cover the pot, reduce to low heat, and simmer the broth for 3 hours
  8. Now, place your pork shoulder or belly into the pot, and simmer for 30 - 45 minutes
  9. In a separate pot will be the tare - Mix together Ginger - 3/4 cup sake - 3/4 cup mirin - 1 & 1/2 cup soy sauce
  10. Bring the tare to a boil over high heat for 5-7 minutes
  11. Once both the pork shoulder/belly and tare are ready, spread the tare sparingly over the meat
  12. Afterwards, cool the pork in a refrigerator
  13. Drain out the ramen broth into a bowl. It’s up to you whether you’d like to save or throw away the rest of the bones and fragrance vegetables. - Decide how much ramen you are going to serve, and keep an appropriate amount of broth simmering over the stove
  14. Take out the pork. You may cut to your desire. Spread the tare over each cut tenderly and sparingly. We just want to bring out a little color in the pork.
  15. Bring out another bowl, fill it with water, and bring to a boil
  16. Take your noodles, and prepare them according to the instructions on the bag. Be mindful of how soft or firm you want your noodles
  17. In a ramen bowl, add: - 1/4 to 1/3 cup tare (depending on how salty you’d like it) - 1-2 cups of broth, I usually just eyeball this
  18. Add your noodles
  19. Add your garnish: egg, green onions
  20. Enjoy!
